Jollof Rice Recipe

Jollof Rice is a popular dish made in various countries in West Africa. I am Nigerian, so the type I will be teaching you how to make is Nigerian Jollof Rice! It is made with common rice, tomatoes, peppers, and a mix of aromatic spices.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 red bell peppers, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt & Pepper if you like

Instructions:

  1. Wash the rice thoroughly and set it aside to drain.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  3. Add chopped onions and saut√© until translucent.
  4. Add minced garlic, chopped red bell peppers, and tomatoes. Cook until the vegetables are soft.
  5. Stir in thyme, curry powder, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Cook for another 2 minutes.
  6. Add the washed rice to the pot and stir to coat it with the vegetable and spice mixture.
  7. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and bring to a boil.
  8. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for 20-25 minutes or until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  9. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Remove from heat, fluff the rice with a fork, and serve hot.

You are done! Time to dig-in to your sumptuous Jollof Rice!
